# Sample Questions for MentorBot

Use these questions to test the system and demonstrate how MentorBot helps new hires get instant answers!

---

## Getting Started Questions

### About Your Role
- Who is my manager?
- What are my main responsibilities as a Junior Data Scientist?
- What are the success metrics for my first 90 days?
- Who is my mentor?

### Team & Contacts
- Who are the members of my team?
- Who should I contact for data access requests?
- Who is the Product Manager I'll be working with?
- How do I reach IT support?

### First Week
- What should I do on my first day?
- What training do I need to complete in my first two weeks?
- What meetings should I attend?

---

## Workflow Questions

### Data Access
- How do I request access to data?
- How long does it take to get data access approved?
- Who approves data access requests?
- Can I access production data?

### A/B Testing

- Where can I find the A/B test SQL template?
- Who do I share A/B test results with?
- What statistical methods should I use for A/B testing?

### Model Deployment
- How do I deploy a machine learning model to production?
- Who needs to approve production deployments?
- What's the process for model code review?
- How do I monitor models in production?

### Dashboards
- How do I create a dashboard?
- What dashboard tool should I use?
- Who can help me with dashboard design?
- Where should I publish dashboards?

## Complex Scenario Questions

- I need to analyze an A/B test for a new checkout flow. What's the complete step-by-step process?
- I want to build and deploy a churn prediction model. What are all the steps involved?
- I need to create a dashboard for the marketing team. How do I go from requirements to published dashboard?
- I have an idea for a conference talk. What's the approval process and budget?
- I accidentally ran a query that affected production. What should I do immediately?

---

**Pro Tip**: Try asking follow-up questions! MentorBot understands context from the uploaded documents and can provide detailed, specific answers based on your company's actual processes and contacts.

**Example Conversation Flow**:
1. "Who is my manager?"
2. "What should I discuss with Sarah in my 1:1 meetings?"
3. "How often do we meet?"
4. "When are her office hours?"
